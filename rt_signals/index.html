In this section, we use various measures of social distancing as additional signals of $R_{s,t}$.

In computing $\Delta \log \Delta C$, we take backward differences of length 7, so

This means that our time $t$ outcome is reflects the average reproductive number of reported cases from time $t-7$ to $t$. If it takes $L$ days for infections to be reported, then these will reflect conditions at time $t-7-L$ to $t-L$. Hence, define, $M_{s,t}$ as the moving average from time $t-7-L$ to $t-L$ of a vector of social distancing metrics. Similarly, for $X_{s,t}$ we use the moving average of the policy indicator variables from time $t-7-L$ to $t-L$. We will focus on $L=10$. (I think Hiro’s and Victor’s regressions are using social distancing metrics at time $t-14$.)
